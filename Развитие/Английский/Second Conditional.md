
> [!note] ❗**The rule** 
> The second conditional is used to talk about hypothetical or unreal situations in the present or future, and their possible outcomes.
> If I won the lottery, I would travel the world.
> 
> The structure is:  
**If + Past Simple, would + infinitive**

Examles:
- If I were (or was) you, I would take that job.
- If she had more time, she would help us.
- If it rained, we would stay indoors.

Would can be substituted with just "'d".

Example:
- If we were rich, we'd travel the world.
